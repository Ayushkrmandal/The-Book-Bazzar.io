Non-Fictional Books

The Wright Brothers

Author: David McCullough

Description: A biography of Wilbur and Orville Wright, detailing how two bicycle mechanics from Ohio defied the odds to invent and pilot the first successful airplane, forever changing history.

The Soul of America: The Battle for Our Better Angels

Author: Jon Meacham

Description: Meacham explores key moments in U.S. history when the country faced division and challenges similar to today, offering lessons from the past to help navigate the present and future.

Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup

Author: John Carreyrou

Description: The story of Theranos, the biotech company founded by Elizabeth Holmes, and how its groundbreaking blood-testing technology turned out to be a massive fraud. Carreyrou’s investigative journalism uncovered the truth behind the company’s rise and fall.

The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History

Author: Elizabeth Kolbert

Description: This book examines the ongoing mass extinction of species caused by human activity and compares it to the five previous mass extinctions in Earth’s history, highlighting the environmental and ecological consequences.

Just Mercy: A Story of Justice and Redemption

Author: Bryan Stevenson

Description: A memoir by attorney Bryan Stevenson, founder of the Equal Justice Initiative, focusing on his work defending marginalized clients, including death row inmates, and his fight for justice in a flawed legal system.

Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance

Author: Angela Duckworth

Description: Duckworth argues that success is driven more by passion and perseverance—grit—than by talent or intelligence. She examines how grit can be cultivated and how it plays a role in achievement in various fields.

Featured Books

To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Genre: Fiction

Description: Set in the American South during the 1930s, this novel explores themes of racial injustice and moral growth through the eyes of a young girl named Scout Finch.

1984

Author: George Orwell

Genre: Dystopian Fiction

Description: A chilling portrayal of a totalitarian regime that exercises extreme control over every aspect of life, focusing on the life of Winston Smith, who seeks truth and freedom.

Pride and Prejudice

Author: Jane Austen

Genre: Romance

Description: This classic novel follows the spirited Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates issues of class, marriage, and morality in early 19th-century England.

The Great Gatsby

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

Genre: Fiction

Description: A tale of ambition and excess in the Roaring Twenties, this novel tells the story of Jay Gatsby and his obsession with the beautiful Daisy Buchanan.

The Catcher in the Rye

Author: J.D. Salinger

Genre: Young Adult Fiction

Description: Following the rebellious teenager Holden Caulfield, this novel captures the disillusionment of adolescence and the challenges of growing up.

Brave New World

Author: Aldous Huxley

Genre: Dystopian Fiction

Description: Set in a futuristic society that values conformity and pleasure over individuality, this novel questions the cost of technological advancement.

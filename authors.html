Authors Highlight

William Shakespeare (1564–1616)

Genre: Drama, Poetry

Notable Works: Hamlet, Romeo and Juliet, Macbeth, A Midsummer Night’s Dream

Description: Often regarded as the greatest writer in the English language, Shakespeare's works span tragedies, comedies, histories, and poems. His profound understanding of human nature, innovative use of language, and complex characters have left an indelible mark on literature, theater, and culture.

Leo Tolstoy (1828–1910)

Genre: Novels, Short Stories, Essays

Notable Works: War and Peace, Anna Karenina, The Death of Ivan Ilyich

Description: A giant of Russian literature, Tolstoy is celebrated for his deeply philosophical novels and his ability to capture the breadth of human experience. His epic novels are considered masterpieces, blending complex characters with reflections on history, war, love, and morality.

Jane Austen (1775–1817)

Genre: Novels

Notable Works: Pride and Prejudice, Emma, Sense and Sensibility, Mansfield Park

Description: Jane Austen is renowned for her keen observations of social manners and relationships in the early 19th-century British gentry. Her novels are filled with wit and irony, focusing on themes of love, marriage, class, and the role of individuals in society.

Gabriel García Márquez (1927–2014)

Genre: Magical Realism, Novels, Short Stories

Notable Works: One Hundred Years of Solitude, Love in the Time of Cholera, Chronicle of a Death Foretold

Description: A key figure in the Latin American literary boom, García Márquez is best known for pioneering the magical realism genre. His works explore themes of solitude, destiny, and the intertwining of history and myth, bringing Latin American literature to international prominence.

Virginia Woolf (1882–1941)

Genre: Modernist Fiction, Essays

Notable Works: Mrs. Dalloway, To the Lighthouse, A Room of One’s Own

Description: Woolf was a key figure in the modernist literary movement. Known for her stream-of-consciousness narration and exploration of characters’ inner lives, Woolf is also celebrated for her essay A Room of One’s Own, advocating for women’s intellectual and creative freedom.

Fictional Books

The Kite Runner

Author: Khaled Hosseini

Description: This poignant tale follows Amir, a boy from Kabul, and his complex friendship with Hassan. It explores themes of betrayal, redemption, and the lasting impact of childhood.

Little Fires Everywhere

Author: Celeste Ng

Description: Set in a suburban community, this novel examines the lives of two families—the Richardsons and the Warrens—whose paths collide, leading to a series of events that uncover secrets and challenge societal norms.

Life After Life

Author: Kate Atkinson

Description: Ursula Todd is born in 1910 and lives multiple lives, experiencing various outcomes each time she dies. The novel explores themes of fate, choice, and the impact of individual lives on history.

The Goldfinch

Author: Donna Tartt

Description: After surviving a terrorist attack in a museum, Theo Decker steals a famous painting. His life spirals into a world of art and crime, reflecting on loss, beauty, and the search for belonging.

Station Eleven

Author: Emily St. John Mandel

Description: Following a global pandemic that wipes out civilization, the story interweaves the lives of survivors, including a traveling Shakespearean troupe, exploring the importance of art and humanity in a post-apocalyptic world.

Americanah

Author: Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Description: A young Nigerian woman, Ifemelu, moves to the United States for college, navigating issues of race, identity, and love. The novel contrasts her experiences in America with her life in Nigeria.

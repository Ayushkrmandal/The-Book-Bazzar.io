Non-Fictional Books

Quiet: The Power of Introverts in a World That Can’t Stop Talking

Author: Susan Cain

Description: Susan Cain argues that society undervalues introverts, and she highlights the strengths and talents that introverted people bring to the world, particularly in leadership, creativity, and innovation.

The Body: A Guide for Occupants

Author: Bill Bryson

Description: A fascinating exploration of the human body, covering everything from cells and organs to the history of medical breakthroughs. Bryson combines humor and science to make complex biology accessible and engaging.

Outliers: The Story of Success

Author: Malcolm Gladwell

Description: Gladwell examines the factors that contribute to high levels of success, arguing that luck, timing, culture, and opportunity often play a larger role than innate talent or intelligence.

Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones

Author: James Clear

Description: Clear presents a practical guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones, using small, incremental changes that lead to significant personal growth and long-term success.

Thinking, Fast and Slow

Author: Daniel Kahneman

Description: Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman explores how humans think and make decisions, distinguishing between two modes of thought: fast, intuitive thinking, and slow, deliberate reasoning.

The Warmth of Other Suns: The Epic Story of America’s Great Migration

Author: Isabel Wilkerson

Description: This historical narrative chronicles the migration of African Americans from the South to the North and West in search of a better life during the early to mid-20th century, focusing on the personal stories of three individuals.
